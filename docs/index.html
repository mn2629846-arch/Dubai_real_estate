Most popular questions

Can foreigners own property in Dubai?

Yes — absolutely. Foreigners can buy freehold property in designated areas (like Downtown, Palm Jumeirah, Dubai Marina). No residency required.

What's the minimum investment to buy a property in Dubai?

The minimum investment varies by area and developer. In some areas, you can start from AED 500,000. Luxury properties may require AED 5M+.

Is there property tax in Dubai?

No, there is no annual property tax for residential properties in Dubai. However, there are transaction fees (4% for buyers, 2% for sellers).

Do I need a visa to buy property?

No, you don’t need a visa to buy property. However, if you invest over AED 2M, you can apply for a Golden Visa.

Are there any hidden fees when buying property?

There are no hidden fees. All costs are transparent: DLD fee (4%), agent commission (if applicable), mortgage registration fee (if applicable).

Can I get a mortgage as a foreigner?

Yes, many banks offer mortgages to foreigners. Loan-to-value ratios range from 50% to 75%, depending on your nationality and income.

How long does it take to complete a property purchase?

Typically 2–4 weeks for off-plan properties, 1–2 weeks for ready properties. The process includes due diligence, contract signing, and DLD registration.

What is the rental yield in Dubai?

Average rental yield is 5–8%. High-yield areas include Downtown, Business Bay, and Palm Jumeirah. Yield depends on location, property type, and market conditions.

Can I rent out my property?

Yes, you can rent out your property. Dubai has a strong rental market, especially for short-term rentals (Airbnb) in tourist areas.

What happens if the developer delays delivery?

If the developer delays delivery, you can claim compensation as per the sales agreement. Many developers also offer flexible payment plans or alternative units.
